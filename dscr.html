DSCR Rental Loans

DSCR loans that qualify on the property’s income.

Rental-property financing underwritten on the asset’s debt-service coverage — not your personal income — for investors building a portfolio, in all fifty states.

The Program

A DSCR loan asks one question: does the rent cover the debt? The property qualifies itself — no tax returns, no income verification.

Overview

Underwrite the asset, not the borrower.

A DSCR loan is a rental-property loan qualified on the Debt-Service Coverage Ratio — the property’s income measured against its debt payments. When the asset covers its own obligations, it qualifies. That makes DSCR financing well suited to investors whose personal tax returns understate their capacity, and to anyone who wants to keep personal income out of the file entirely.

It is a clean, repeatable way to finance and scale a rental portfolio, available on single assets and across all fifty states. Loan amounts run from $100K to $5M and beyond on single-family rentals, 2–4 unit properties, and small multifamily. For owner-occupied or larger commercial property, a commercial loan or SBA loan is usually the better fit.

The Ratio

How DSCR is calculated.

DSCR is the property’s net operating income divided by its annual debt service (principal and interest). A ratio of 1.0x means the rent exactly covers the payment; above 1.0x means the property produces surplus cash; below 1.0x means it runs short. Lenders typically look for a DSCR in the range of 1.0x to 1.25x, though the threshold varies by program and leverage.

For example — figures illustrative only — a rental with $60,000 of net operating income and $48,000 of annual debt payments has a DSCR of 1.25x ($60,000 ÷ $48,000). The stronger the ratio, the more financing options open up. We calculate your property’s DSCR up front so you know where it stands before you apply.

Questions

Frequently asked questions

What is a DSCR loan?

A DSCR loan is a rental-property loan qualified on the property’s Debt-Service Coverage Ratio — its income versus its debt payments — instead of the borrower’s personal income. If the rent covers the loan payment at the required ratio, the property qualifies, with no tax returns or income verification.

How is DSCR calculated?

For 1–4 unit rentals, DSCR is the monthly rent divided by the full monthly payment — principal, interest, taxes, insurance, and any HOA (PITIA). For example, $3,600 rent against a $3,067 full payment is a DSCR of 1.17x. (Figures illustrative.)

What DSCR do I need to qualify?

Programs generally look for a DSCR in the range of 1.0x to 1.25x, though the exact minimum depends on leverage, property type, and the specific loan. A higher ratio typically opens up more options and better structure. We calculate your property’s ratio before you apply so there are no surprises.

Do DSCR loans require income verification or tax returns?

No. That is the defining feature — DSCR loans are underwritten on the property’s cash flow, so they do not require personal income verification or tax returns. This makes them well suited to self-employed investors whose returns understate their real capacity.

What properties qualify for a DSCR loan?

DSCR financing typically covers single-family rentals, 2–4 unit properties, and small multifamily held as investments, with loan amounts from $100K to $5M+. Owner-occupied homes do not qualify, since the program is built for rental investment property.

Can I use a DSCR loan to grow a rental portfolio?

Yes. Because each loan is qualified on the individual property’s cash flow rather than your personal income, DSCR financing scales cleanly across multiple properties — a common way investors build and refinance a portfolio in all fifty states.
