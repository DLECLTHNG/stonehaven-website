Straight Answer

When DSCR may not be the best option.

Part of evaluating a deal is knowing when a different structure wins. Another path is often better when the investor qualifies for materially cheaper conventional financing; when the property's economic cash flow is weak even if the formula passes; when the planned exit conflicts with a prepayment penalty; when major rehabilitation is needed before permanent financing; when rent, zoning or insurance is uncertain; when the home will be owner-occupied; or when required equity or reserves aren't there yet.

If one of those describes your deal, that's exactly what the review is for - we'll say so, and show the alternative.

Questions

Frequently asked questions

What is a DSCR loan?

A DSCR loan is investment-property financing that evaluates eligible property rental income against the proposed property payment. Qualified investors may finance eligible rental properties without relying primarily on traditional employment-income documentation. It is business-purpose financing for investment property only.

How is residential DSCR calculated?

For 1-4 unit rentals, most programs divide gross monthly rent by the full qualifying payment - principal, interest, taxes, insurance and any HOA (PITIA). Illustratively, $3,000 rent against a $2,647 payment is a 1.13x DSCR. Methodology varies by program.

What does a 1.00 DSCR mean?

The rent exactly covers the qualifying payment - no cushion. Above 1.00x the property produces surplus under the formula; below 1.00x it runs short at those terms.

Is DSCR based on gross rent or NOI?

Residential DSCR typically uses gross rent ÷ PITIA. Commercial DSCR uses NOI ÷ annual debt service - a stricter test that nets out operating expenses first. Our analysis shows both views so the difference is visible.

Does passing DSCR mean the property is profitable?

No. The lender formula ignores vacancy, management, maintenance and capital reserves. A deal can qualify and still produce thin or negative real cash flow - which is exactly why we present an investor view alongside the lender view.

Are tax returns required?

Typically not for income qualification - that is the point of the product. Other documentation is still required: identification, entity documents, assets and reserves, leases and insurance. DSCR is not a "no-doc" loan.

Is credit reviewed?

Yes. Credit is reviewed in every program we're aware of, and the score band typically affects eligibility, pricing and maximum leverage.

Can a first-time investor qualify?

Many programs allow first-time investors, sometimes with adjusted terms. Experience mainly affects pricing, leverage and short-term-rental treatment - varies by program.

Can an LLC obtain the loan?

Yes - vesting in an LLC or similar entity is common in DSCR lending, with the entity's formation documents in the file.

Does an LLC eliminate a personal guarantee?

Usually not. Most residential DSCR programs require a personal guaranty from the members, and holding title in an LLC does not remove all personal liability or credit relevance.

Are short-term rentals eligible?

Some programs consider short-term rental income with a documented history or market data, and may require proof the STR use is legal in that jurisdiction. Treatment varies meaningfully by program.

Can a vacant property qualify?

Often, using the appraiser's market-rent opinion instead of a lease - common on purchases and BRRRR takeouts. Program-dependent.

Can a DSCR loan finance five or more units?

Five-plus units generally moves into small-balance commercial or specialty programs with different underwriting - NOI, debt yield, commercial DSCR. We arrange both sides of that line and will tell you which one your property is on.

Can DSCR be used for cash-out?

Yes, on eligible properties - subject to leverage limits, ownership seasoning and reserve requirements that vary by program.

What is a no-ratio DSCR loan?

A variant where the lender doesn't test the ratio at all, typically traded for lower leverage and higher pricing. Useful in narrow situations; availability varies.

What reserves may be required?

Commonly several months of the full property payment in liquid assets, scaling with loan size, leverage and portfolio - varies by program.

What is a prepayment penalty?

A charge for paying the loan off early - on DSCR loans often a step-down structure over the first years. Some states restrict certain structures. Evaluate it against your planned exit before committing; our explainer models the exposure.

How quickly can a DSCR transaction close?

It depends on appraisal, title, insurance and entity documentation. We set a realistic timeline per deal at review rather than promising a universal number.

Can a DSCR loan finance an owner-occupied property?

No. DSCR is business-purpose financing for investment property. Owner-occupied homes belong in consumer mortgage products with different rules.

How does DSCR compare with conventional financing?

Conventional investment loans underwrite your personal income and DTI and are often cheaper when you qualify. DSCR underwrites the property, scales across a portfolio, and accommodates entity vesting. When conventional is materially cheaper and available to you, we'll say so.

What happens if the appraiser's rent estimate is low?

The qualifying rent - and the ratio - drops. Options include restructuring leverage, interest-only qualification where offered, contesting the analysis with better comparables, or re-scoping the deal. Identifying that risk early is a core purpose of the review.

Can Stonehaven help with commercial real estate financing?

Yes - commercial debt placement is the other half of the desk, from small multifamily through larger commercial assets. See commercial financing.
